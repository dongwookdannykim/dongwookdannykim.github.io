Professional Summary

I study how states create, justify, and stabilize claims of right over legally open global commons. My dissertation and first-book project, “Authority without Territory: State Claims of Right in the Global Commons,” asks why some such claims stabilize into accepted rights while others fail. My work draws on historical, legal, policy, and multi-method evidence, with methodological training spanning qualitative, quantitative, formal, geospatial, and computational approaches.
